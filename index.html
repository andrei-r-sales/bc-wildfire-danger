About this map

A machine-learning estimate of daily wildfire danger (1–5) across British Columbia. It is not an official rating.

How it works

A random-forest model trained on ~560,000 station-days of historical BC Wildfire Service danger ratings, using live weather (Open-Meteo), satellite vegetation & moisture (NASA MODIS), and terrain. It regenerates automatically every morning.

How accurate is it?

Measured honestly on 2024–2025 data the model never trained on: about 55% exact and 95% within one level (quadratic-weighted κ 0.77). Validated against the official rating, it runs only mildly high overall (+0.16 levels) and preserves the correct ordering of risk.

Known limits

  • Coarse ~30 km grid — treat cells as regional, not street-level.
  • Coastal & urban areas (dashed zone) have sparse training data and run ~0.35 levels high — least reliable there.

For real decisions

Always defer to the official BC Wildfire Service rating.
